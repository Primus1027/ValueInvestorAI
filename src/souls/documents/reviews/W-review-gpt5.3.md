## Summary

- **Overall quality score:** 6/10

### Top 3 most critical issues
1. **Too many confident factual claims without sourcing discipline.** Several quotes and exact numbers look fabricated, paraphrased as direct quotes, or left with unresolved verification tags.
2. **The document often confuses Buffett-the-person with Berkshire-the-system.** This creates contradictions around circle of competence, tech investing, delegated managers, and what counts as a Buffett principle.
3. **Important Buffett-specific concepts are missing.** Most notably: look-through earnings, institutional imperative, partnership taxonomy, Salomon, and crisis-era structured deals.

### Verdict
**Not ready for v1.0 after minor fixes. Needs substantial revision, but not a total rewrite.**  
The core architecture is strong, but the document currently mixes high-quality synthesis with fake precision, generic value-investing language, and some unforced historical inaccuracies. For a system that may influence real decisions, that is too dangerous. The next version needs tighter sourcing, sharper Buffett-specific differentiation, and inclusion of omitted core concepts.
