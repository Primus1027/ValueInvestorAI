## Problem Statement

Build an AI system that makes autonomous value investment decisions capable of beating the index over 10 years. The system produces daily content about value investing and makes specific stock picks with prices and portfolio allocation. The core thesis: AI's edge in value investing is emotional neutrality AND research stamina — it never panics during crashes and never gets bored analyzing the 200th annual report.

## What Makes This Cool

The insight everyone misses: AI investing doesn't have to mean quantitative/short-term trading. Long-term value investing — the Buffett playbook — is actually where AI has the BIGGEST advantage, because the hardest part isn't the math. It's the temperament. AI gets temperament for free.

Second layer: most "value investors" fail before they even trade, because they get tired of doing deep research and make decisions based on incomplete analysis. AI eliminates research fatigue entirely.

The 10x vision: a completely AI-run Berkshire Hathaway.

### Cost Model
Value investing is NOT a brute-force scan of all companies. The real process is a funnel:

1. **Qualitative filter (near-zero cost):** Masters' frameworks eliminate ~95% of companies upfront — no moat, bad management, outside circle of competence, wrong industry. This is rule-based, not LLM-based.
2. **Deep analysis (LLM cost):** Only the ~20-50 companies that pass the qualitative filter get full multi-master analysis. This happens once per company, then updates only on material events (earnings, management change, price drop to interesting range).
3. **Ongoing scan (low cost):** Periodic lightweight scan for companies that newly fall into the investable range (price drops, business improvements, new IPOs).

Cost reality: most months, only 2-5 new companies need deep analysis. Budget: ~$50-100/month for LLM APIs. The expensive "5 candidates x 4 masters daily" model doesn't reflect how value investors actually work — they build a watchlist, go deep on a few, and wait.

## Premises

1. AI can learn and consistently apply value investing principles (buy below intrinsic value, hold long-term, ignore market noise)
2. Humans fail at value investing due to emotions (fear/greed) AND research fatigue (boredom/overwhelm) — AI eliminates both
3. Daily content production and investment decisions are synergistic — writing is articulating the investment logic
4. Founder skips virtual portfolio, goes to real money ($100K) with human review gate on every buy/sell — reviewing AI reasoning IS the validation method
5. Memory architecture is the critical technical challenge — AI must learn what to remember and what to forget, like a talented human investor
6. Multiple masters add value NOT through consensus but through divergence detection — disagreements force deeper analysis
7. The investment PROCESS is the product from day one — even if early analysis isn't sharp, the visible self-improvement journey is compelling

## Recommended Approach: Soul-First Build Path

### Phase 0: Foundation (Week 1, 3-day spike)
**Memory architecture decision.** This is the load-bearing technical unknown.

Spike scope (3 days, time-boxed):
- Evaluate: RAG with vector store, structured knowledge graph, hybrid approach
- Decision tree: if RAG sufficient for thesis tracking → proceed with RAG; if knowledge graph needed → add 1-2 weeks to Phase 1; if fine-tuning needed → flag cost impact and defer
- Deliverable: working read/write memory interface — agents can store observations and retrieve relevant context
- Memory interface contract:
  - **Write triggers:** after every company analysis, after every market event assessment, after every investment decision
  - **Read interface:** at analysis time, master agent receives relevant prior observations about the company, sector, and related theses
  - **Importance scoring:** recency + relevance + thesis-impact weighting (details TBD in spike)

Tech stack selection and project initialization happens in parallel.

**Phase 0 exit criteria:**
- [ ] Memory interface implemented with read/write working
- [ ] Tech stack chosen, repo initialized, data models defined
- [ ] One master (Buffett) can store and retrieve a company analysis across sessions — retrieved analysis includes: company name, valuation range, key moat factor, date of analysis; retrieval completes in <10 seconds
- [ ] 5 calibration cases identified with documented real master positions (sourced in parallel with memory spike)

### Phase 1: Decision-Style Modeling (Week 2-4)
Build all 4 master decision frameworks in parallel. Note: "soul packaging" is the internal working concept; external framing ("投资决策风格仿真" vs "灵魂封装") is a separate branding decision to be made before any public content.

**Buffett:**
- Source: Shareholder letters 1957-2025 (Berkshire website PDF), CNBC Archive transcripts
- Framework: Owner earnings, moat analysis (brand/cost/network/switching), management quality, margin of safety, circle of competence
- Distinctive trait: "Wonderful company at a fair price" over "fair company at a wonderful price"

**Munger:**
- Source: 千页合集 PDF (primary), public speech transcripts
- Framework: Mental models lattice, inversion thinking, psychology of misjudgment, checklist approach
- Distinctive trait: "Avoid stupidity" as primary filter — the kill switch agent
- Note: Stripe Press online version is not reliably available for programmatic ingestion; use PDF sources

**Li Lu:**
- Source: Columbia University speeches (2006/2010/2021) transcripts
- Framework: Knowledge compounding, emerging market structural growth, China modernization thesis
- Distinctive trait: Long-duration thesis on civilization-level trends

**Duan Yongping:**
- Source: 段永平投资问答录 (Xueqiu special edition PDF)
- Framework: Business model simplicity, "do the right thing", consumer brand evaluation
- Distinctive trait: Chinese consumer/tech company expertise, extreme patience

**Calibration protocol:**
Select 5 historical cases where real masters publicly diverged (e.g., Buffett bought airline stocks, Munger was skeptical). Run each AI master through these cases. Pass criteria: AI master's directional recommendation matches the real master's actual position in ≥4 of 5 cases.

**Phase 1 exit criteria:**
- [ ] All 4 masters can analyze the same company and produce measurably different perspectives
- [ ] Each master identifies ≥3 framework-specific factors not in a generic checklist
- [ ] Each master proposes a valuation range with explicitly stated assumptions
- [ ] Calibration test: ≥4/5 historical cases match real master's actual position direction
- [ ] Memory system retains and retrieves prior analyses across sessions

### Phase 2: Weekly Analysis Engine (Week 5-8)
Note: starts after Phase 1 masters reach minimum viable quality (calibration passed).

- **Qualitative filter pipeline:** rule-based screening using masters' frameworks (moat? management? circle of competence?) — eliminates ~95% of companies, near-zero LLM cost
- **Deep analysis:** 2-5 companies per month that pass the filter, full multi-master analysis (Claude/GPT for Buffett/Munger, Doubao for Duan/Li Lu)
- **Ongoing scan:** periodic lightweight check for companies newly falling into investable range (price drops, earnings surprises, new IPOs) — yfinance + SEC EDGAR + Finnhub
- Content generation: 1 article per analysis, 1000-2000 words, structure: thesis + evidence + master-specific perspectives + areas of disagreement
- Content review: founder reviews before any publishing; automated quality threshold for draft generation
- Decision logging: structured JSON audit trail of every analysis (company, date, master, recommendation, confidence, key factors, valuation range)

**When masters disagree in content:** highlight the disagreement explicitly as the most interesting part of the article. "Buffett would buy at this price because X. Munger would pass because Y." The tension IS the content.

**Phase 2 exit criteria:**
- [ ] Weekly analysis pipeline runs end-to-end for 3 consecutive weeks
- [ ] ≥10 company analyses completed with specific recommendations
- [ ] Content generation produces articles with all required sections (thesis, evidence, master perspectives, disagreement highlight), 1000-2000 words, passes founder review within 2 rounds of revision
- [ ] Decision audit trail captures every analysis in queryable format

### Phase 3: Investment Decisions (Week 8+)
**Gated on:** founder's personal legal comfort level with investing based on AI analysis. Compliance opinion recommended but not blocking for personal investment of own capital.

- Stock picks with specific entry prices and portfolio allocation %
- Human approval gate (founder reviews every buy/sell — the reasoning review IS the validation)
- Position tracking and thesis monitoring (manual review of thesis status weekly)
- Thesis-breaking alert system deferred to Phase 4 (requires event monitoring infrastructure; out of scope for Phase 3)
- Doubao integration: if added in Phase 2, used here for Chinese market analysis alongside Claude

## Failure Modes

| Failure | Impact | Mitigation |
|---------|--------|------------|
| Data API down (yfinance/EDGAR) | Analysis pipeline stalls | Fallback to cached data (24h stale acceptable for value investing) |
| LLM hallucinated ticker/data | Bad analysis, potential bad investment | Cross-check all tickers and financial data against source APIs before presenting to human |
| Content below quality threshold | Unpublishable article | Skip publish, log for review; founder approval gate catches this |
| Memory system loses context | Masters restart fresh, lose accumulated learning | Daily backup of memory store; periodic integrity checks |
| Cost overrun | Monthly budget exceeded | Hard spending cap with alerts; degrade to weekly-only analysis |
